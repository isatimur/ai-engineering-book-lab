## Cao 2026 — Agentic Software

- **Title:** Agentic Software: How AI Agents Are Restructuring the Software Paradigm
- **Author:** Zhenfeng Cao
- **Source:** arXiv:2606.05608 (cs.SE, cs.AI) — https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05608
- **Date:** submitted 2026-06-04, revised 2026-06-10
- **Summary:** Argues AI agents (LLMs as reasoning engines, dynamically
  generating code) are a shift in software itself, not a tooling advance;
  traces licensed software → SaaS → Agent-as-a-Service; recasts the human role
  as "intent architect." Section 7.3 ("For Organizations") recommends
  identifying agent-ready workflows, investing in evaluation frameworks, and
  restructuring toward smaller teams of "agent orchestrators."
- **Relation to the book:** Convergent on evals-as-the-signal (Ch4) and on the
  general organizational transition (Ch9); divergent on team topology — the
  paper predicts headcount shape ("smaller teams of agent orchestrators"),
  the book argues for function shifts (judgment scarcity, review bottleneck,
  broader creation with narrower shipping paths). Full point-by-point
  comparison: [docs/positioning-notes.md](../docs/positioning-notes.md).
- **Candidate chapters:** 1, 3, 4, 9
- **Support relationship:** corroborating for the delegation thesis (Ch1) and
  eval-signal claim (Ch4); counterpoint for the team-structure claims (Ch9).
