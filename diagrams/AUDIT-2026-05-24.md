# Diagram Audit — 2026-05-24

## Summary
64 diagrams audited against book source content (chapter packets, source-backed outline v2, concept docs, theme map). Overall posture: structural / chapter-card / inline-figure diagrams are factually solid; the largest cluster of errors is in **concept cards** where the "Appears in: ch X, ch Y" chapter-mapping line is wrong for 6 of 18 cards. Two attribution errors are concrete enough to fix (Kwindla→Luke Harries on the speech-layer wrapper; Perszyk→Tejas Kumar on the 5-input taxonomy). Numeric claims (693 videos, 10 themes, 18 concepts, 17.6% Intercom, 2x throughput, theme counts) all verified clean.

Findings: 4 HIGH, 8 MEDIUM, 12 LOW (wording drift), 5 notable omissions.

## High-severity findings

**1. `concepts/04-ai-native-organization.excalidraw` — wrong chapter mapping**
Card says "Appears in: ch 7, ch 8". Source: this is the anchor concept of Ch 9 ("The AI-Native Organization"). Concept doc explicitly says it "anchors the back third of the book." Fix → `Appears in: ch 9`.

**2. `concepts/18-voice-agents.excalidraw` — wrong chapter mapping**
Card says "Appears in: ch 5, ch 7". Source: Voice Agents is the central concept of Ch 8 (Realtime and Embodied Edges). All Voice Agents source cluster videos (#26, #142, #145, #146) live in Ch 8 packet. Fix → `Appears in: ch 8`.

**3. `inline/ch08-fig2-speech-layer-wrapper.excalidraw` — misattribution**
Caption: "The durable pattern: a thin speech layer around an agent that already works. — Kwindla Hultman Kramer, Daily / Pipecat · corpus video #142". Source: this "wrap a proven text agent with a voice engine" pattern is Luke Harries's claim in #661, not Kwindla's #142 (which is the Pipecat Cloud talk). Voice Agents concept doc explicitly attributes the wrapper argument to Luke Harries. Fix → attribute to Luke Harries, ElevenLabs · #661.

**4. `inline/ch10-fig2-constrained-delegation.excalidraw` — misattribution + framing not in source**
Caption: "Five inputs — task, environment, context, state, narrow powers — convert capability into trust. — Danielle Perszyk, Research Scientist, Amazon AGI · corpus video #136". Source: the Constrained Delegation concept doc names a "five-component taxonomy" and attributes it to Tejas Kumar (#690), not Perszyk. Perszyk's #136 (Useful General Intelligence) is in Ch 10 but is not the source of the 5-input framing. The concept's own structural argument is the reliability triad (scope / contract / adversarial eval), not five inputs. Fix → either re-attribute to Tejas Kumar (#690) or swap the figure to the triad framing used elsewhere in the book.

## Medium-severity findings

**5. `concepts/01-agentic-product-design.excalidraw` — wrong chapter mapping**
Card says "Appears in: ch 5, ch 7". Concept is the UX expression of constrained delegation (Ch 1) and the human control plane (Ch 6). Fix → `Appears in: ch 1, ch 6`.

**6. `concepts/09-graphrag.excalidraw` — wrong chapter mapping**
Card says "Appears in: ch 4, ch 8". GraphRAG is treated entirely as a Ch 5 (Context Is Infrastructure) sub-pattern in the outline and concept doc. The ch05-fig2 inline figure correctly puts it in Ch 5. Fix → `Appears in: ch 5`.

**7. `concepts/12-human-control-plane.excalidraw` — wrong chapter mapping**
Card says "Appears in: ch 7, ch 10". The Human Control Plane is the **title concept of Ch 6** ("Runtimes, State, and the Human Control Plane"). Concept doc lists Ch 6 sources (#198, #201, #176) prominently. Fix → `Appears in: ch 6` (acceptable to add ch 7 audit angle).

**8. `concepts/15-software-factory.excalidraw` — wrong chapter mapping**
Card says "Appears in: ch 6, ch 7". Outline puts #629 (Cursor software factory) in Ch 3 and Ch 9. Ch 3 packet explicitly names "software factory" in its strongest claim #5. Concept doc itself says "Chapter 6" but this is an internal doc error — the chapter outline does not have a software-factory chapter. Fix → `Appears in: ch 3, ch 9`.

**9. `concepts/02-agent-observability.excalidraw` — chapter mapping partial miss**
Card says "Appears in: ch 4, ch 6, ch 7". Defensible — observability bridges all three. But Ch 4 is the strongest home (concept doc: "turns Chapter 4's argument about evals into an operational practice"). Acceptable.

**10. `04-theme-corpus-map.excalidraw` — General AI Engineering → Ch 10 mapping is weak**
Map ties "General AI Engineering" theme (7 videos: music, transformers→vision, sovereignty, Spotify personalization, AI UX, Downing St rewiring) to Ch 10 ("What Endures"). Source content of those 7 videos has near-zero overlap with Ch 10's strongest claims (constrained delegation, durable principles, bitter lesson). The map's own framing ("Background — not a book chapter") already exists for Models & Inference; General AI Engineering is a better fit for that bucket. Fix → relabel General AI Engineering as background, not Ch 10.

**11. `01-book-argument-spine.excalidraw` — Act III/IV labels under-claim Ch 5**
Spine lumps Ch 3–7 as "Act II · The Scaffolding Stack" but the source-backed outline v2 narrative spine separates them: Ch 3–4 are "the scaffolding" and Ch 5–7 are "the expansion." Reasonable simplification but loses the outline's intended pacing.

**12. `inline/ch08-fig1-latency-budget.excalidraw` vs `concepts/18-voice-agents.excalidraw` — internally inconsistent latency numbers**
Both diagrams target an ~800 ms turn budget but break it down differently:
- ch08-fig1: 120 + 140 + 110 + 330 + 70 + 30 = 800
- concept 18: 150 + 300 + 200 + 120 = 770
Neither breakdown is sourced in concept doc, chapter packet, or Voice Agents concept doc — they are synthesized illustrations. Pick one canonical breakdown and reuse it.

- **Ch 4 chapter card** misses Samuel Colvin's "human-seeded evals" (#184) — chapter packet flags this as strongest claim #3.
- **Ch 6 chapter card** misses Cornelia Davis / Temporal (#38) — the canonical durable-execution voice; gets one quote from Colvin only.
- **Ch 7 chapter card** misses Karan Sampath / Anthropic (#624) on "root of trust" — chapter packet flags this as a top quote and one of the strongest enterprise-MCP claims.
- **Ch 9 chapter card** uses Dan Shipper's 10x quote but misses Maggie Appleton's "alignment debt" framing as a named concept — the chapter packet flags alignment debt as an open question/strongest claim. (The diagram does mention "alignment debt" elsewhere; just not foregrounded.)
