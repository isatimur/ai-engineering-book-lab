## Overview

Gene Kim is the author of The Phoenix Project, The DevOps Handbook, and Wiring the Winning Organization. He brings a decades-long organizational transformation lens to AI engineering — focused on flow, constraints, and feedback loops rather than individual tool adoption.

## Appearances in corpus

- [[084-7Dtu2bilcFs-2026-the-year-the-ide-died-steve-yegge-gene-kim-authors-vibe-coding|#84 — 2026: The Year The IDE Died]] (Coding Agents, Org Design, with Steve Yegge)

## Key claims / positions

- The AI-augmented engineering transformation is structurally similar to the DevOps transformation — the constraint moves, it doesn't disappear.
- Flow metrics (lead time, deployment frequency, change failure rate) will remain the right measures even as AI changes who does the work.
- The bottleneck will shift from coding to requirements, testing, and organizational decision-making as AI absorbs implementation work.
- Organizations that don't invest in feedback loop infrastructure will not benefit from AI tooling.
