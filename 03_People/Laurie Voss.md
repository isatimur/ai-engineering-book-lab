## Overview

Laurie Voss has been a consistent voice in the AI engineering community on the practical side of building and evaluating production agents. Formerly prominent in the JavaScript ecosystem (co-founder of npm), he transitioned to AI engineering advocacy with a practitioner-first, measurement-first philosophy.

## Key claims / positions

- Agent design patterns (ReAct, plan-and-execute, reflection) are means to an end; pick by what your evals reward, not by what sounds sophisticated.
- Most teams skip evals and regret it; evals are the cheapest insurance you can buy before production.
- "Shipping real agents" requires a measurable definition of success before writing any code.
- Observability for agentic systems differs from classic APM — you need trace-level context, not just metrics.
