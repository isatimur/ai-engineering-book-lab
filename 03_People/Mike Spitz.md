# Mike Spitz

**Role:** Engineering Leader, PFF (Pro Football Focus)  
**Organization:** PFF  
**Themes:** [[Org Design & Leadership]], [[Coding Agents]]

## Overview

Mike Spitz represents the "post-engineer engineering org" perspective from a data-intensive sports analytics company. His talk at AI Engineer 2026 is a candid account of what happens to an engineering team's structure, rituals, and role definitions when AI handles a significant fraction of implementation work.

## Appearances in corpus

## Key claims / positions

- Standard engineering rituals (standups, sprint reviews, story points) were designed around human cognitive capacity and don't transfer to AI-augmented teams.
- The "post-engineer" org doesn't eliminate engineers — it changes what they coordinate, review, and are accountable for.
- Agents don't do standups, but someone still has to account for what they shipped — accountability structures must evolve.
- Data-heavy domains (like sports analytics) see faster ROI from AI agents because the tasks are well-defined and measurable.
