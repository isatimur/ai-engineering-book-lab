# Patrick Debois

**Role:** Independent Consultant / DevOps Pioneer  
**Themes:** [[Org Design & Leadership]], [[Coding Agents]]

## Overview

Patrick Debois coined the term "DevOps" and has spent two decades thinking about how software engineering organizations adapt to new tooling paradigms. In the AI engineering era he brings that organizational lens to AI-native development patterns, asking how teams should restructure, not just which tools to adopt.

## Key claims / positions

- Four patterns describe AI-native development: augmentation, automation, agentic, autonomous — each with different organizational implications.
- Reverse Conway's Law: AI agents will reshape organizational communication structures, not just execute within them.
- DevOps was about breaking the dev/ops wall; the next wall to break is human/agent task allocation.
- Organizations that treat AI as tooling (not team members) will hit a ceiling; those that redesign workflows around agent capabilities will not.
