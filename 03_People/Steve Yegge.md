# Steve Yegge

**Role:** Author; formerly Sourcegraph, Google, Amazon  
**Organizations:** Sourcegraph (ex), Google (ex), Amazon (ex)  
**Themes:** [[Coding Agents]], [[Org Design & Leadership]]

## Overview

Steve Yegge is a veteran software engineer and blogger known for influential essays on software engineering culture (notably the "Platforms" rant). In AI engineering he re-emerged as a provocateur arguing that the IDE as a concept is obsolete and that AI coding agents represent a fundamental discontinuity, not an incremental improvement.

## Appearances in corpus

- [[084-7Dtu2bilcFs-2026-the-year-the-ide-died-steve-yegge-gene-kim-authors-vibe-coding|#84 — 2026: The Year The IDE Died]] (Coding Agents, with Gene Kim)

## Key claims / positions

- The IDE is a 40-year-old abstraction designed for humans typing code; agents don't need it and it constrains them.
- "Vibe coding" represents the first genuine paradigm shift in programming since object-oriented programming.
- The programming skill that will matter is intent specification, not syntax mastery.
- Most senior engineers are underestimating how fast the shift will be — the 2026 horizon is real, not hyperbole.
