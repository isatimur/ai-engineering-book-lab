# Voice AI: when is the "Her" moment? — Neil Zeghidour, Gradium AI

## Summary
Neil Zeghidour gives a sober state-of-the-field view of voice AI. His central argument is that the cinematic "Her" experience is still blocked less by raw model quality than by conversational systems problems: end-to-end latency, tool-call delays, inability to support overlap and backchannels, fragile interruption behavior, and weak handling of paralinguistic signals like emotion or hesitation. He also notes a strategic shift: once speech synthesis and recognition get faster and cheaper, the bottleneck moves to orchestration and real-time interaction design.

## Why it matters
- Sharpens the book's broader thesis that realtime AI exposes hidden systems constraints more brutally than text chat does.
- Provides strong evidence that latency and turn-taking are core architecture problems, not polish.
- Adds a useful tension: speech-to-speech lowers latency, but current systems still struggle to achieve human-like conversational flow.
