## Why

Each event ledger is a source-anchored, claim-by-claim treatment of one speaker's
talk. That is flattering, rigorous coverage — the kind a speaker shares. One share
from any of these people reaches precisely the book's audience. This is the
highest-leverage, zero-cost launch channel.

## Message template

Channel: X DM or LinkedIn message, whichever profile is active. Email only if a
public address exists. One message, one follow-up after ~10 days, then stop.

> Hi {name} — I'm writing *From Copilot to Colleague*
> (fromcopilottocolleague.com), an evidence-first book on AI engineering. Your
> talk "{talk}" was strong enough source material that I built it a dedicated
> event ledger: every claim I use from it, anchored to the moment in the video —
> {ledger_url}.
>
> Two things: (1) if I've misread anything you said, tell me and I'll fix it —
> the ledger is the correction surface; (2) if it represents you well, a share
> would mean a lot. Either way, thanks for the talk.

Notes on the template:
- The correction offer is genuine, not a gimmick — it matches the claims-ledger
  model (every claim carries a support level and can be revised).
- Don't ask for a book plug; ask about **their** ledger page. Sharing their own
  coverage is a much easier yes.
