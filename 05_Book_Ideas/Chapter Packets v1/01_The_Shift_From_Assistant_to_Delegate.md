# Chapter 1 — The Shift: From Assistant to Delegate

## Role in the book
Open the book with the real transition: AI stops being a better autocomplete and becomes a candidate worker. The chapter's job is to define the book's central question: what must be true before teams can trust AI to do delegated work?

## Strongest claims
1. The important AI transition is from suggestion to delegated execution.
2. Chat alone is an inadequate interface for complex, long-running, or high-stakes work.
3. Reliability comes less from model cleverness than from surrounding scaffolding.
4. The core design problem is trust under action, not delight under conversation.
