# Editorial Method for Public Manuscript Pages

This public manuscript layer is intentionally more cautious than the internal drafting vault.

## What these pages are doing
They synthesize patterns from the project corpus — especially conference talks, chapter packets, claims ledgers, and evidence packs — into readable chapter prose.

## What they are not doing
They are **not** presenting conference talks as if they were the same thing as peer-reviewed proof or broad quantitative validation.

In practice, claims in the public manuscript should be read in four tiers:

1. **Practitioner convergence** — many builders report a similar pattern or design constraint.
2. **Case-based evidence** — a specific workflow, product, or company presentation demonstrates the pattern in context.
3. **Durable design principle** — a claim is stable because it follows from familiar engineering constraints such as least privilege, observability, or bounded authority.
4. **Provisional frontier claim** — a claim may be directionally important, but the evidence is still thin, fast-moving, or hype-prone.

## Default editorial posture
When evidence is mostly conference-talk level, public prose should:
- say that the corpus shows a pattern, convergence, or repeated lesson
- avoid sounding like the matter is fully settled
- distinguish architecture instincts from demonstrated universal results
- prefer concrete workflow examples over inflated generalization
- leave room for later revision as the field matures

## Why keep this note public
The project is partly an experiment in turning a fast-moving AI engineering discourse into a book-length argument without pretending that all source material carries the same epistemic weight.
